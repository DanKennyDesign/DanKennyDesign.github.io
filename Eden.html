Eden is an interactive display built for the digital wall in Sydney's Central Park One building.

Click here to see the artwork in action (Works best on newer computers)
...Or click here to see a documentary video of the Mind Paintings exhibition



The brief asked us to create digital artworks that could interface with the Neurosky Mindwave headset.

This headset is a portable Electro-Encephalogram (EEG) device that allowed users to interact using their brainwaves.

The device specialises in measuring attention and meditation in the mind.

The Mind Paintings exhibition was a collaboration between the University of Sydney and Ramus Illumination, with the intention of producing works that encourage mindfulness and calm.

Eden was an attempt to use code to simulate a natural environment.

Using the Processing language, I gave the world of Eden its own laws:

An original physics system that allows the wind to blow the plants in the garden,

Functions that built totally unique trees and flowers for each user based on their brainwaves, and

Interaction with the mindwave headset, so that users had to calm the wind with meditation and focus to increase the sunlight ad grow their garden.
